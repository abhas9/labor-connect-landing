Five women die every hour during childbirth in India. It accounts for 17% of such deaths globally. Over 99% of maternal deaths occur in developing countries

More than 80% deliveries occur with the assistance of staff nurse in low resource healthcare settings. However, most of them lack training and decision making skills.

We visited 30+ Low resource healthcare settings across India. Discussed with 50+ midwives and 10+ doctors to understand the problem and possible solutions

The Problem

The midwives/nurse monitors multiple parameters during intrapartum period and further take decisions based on the pattern of those parameters. During our research, we have identified that there is a delay in early detection of complications and timely decision for early referral by the midwives/nurses. This overall leads to maternal/neonatal mortality/morbidity.

Existing protocols for monitoring the progress of labor include WHO recommended Partograph. Our study shows that more than 90% of the midwives fill the partograph after delivery, that too for the data recording formality. We have identified several problems in the existing protocols such as:

  • Complicated to use without proper training
  • Time Consuming with capacity constraints in government hospitals
  • Low user friendliness of paper chart
  • Burden of additional record keeping with no apparent real time benefit
  • Prone to error if used manually and without supervision
  • No alarm â€“ when to take data
  • Data entry does not trigger a call to action

Our Solution

Our product LaborConnect is an intelligent labor monitoring tool which helps act as a decision support tool to help midwives/nurses during the intra-partum period to make timely decision and interventions.

Data monitoring Alarm

Increase protocol adherence using timely data montoring alarms. Build in Partograph generation.

Build in Alert

Intelligent alert algorithm for timely intervention.

Easy User interface

Requires least amount of training. Tested with mid-wives in low-resource healthcare settings.

Provided better health-care to over thousand mothers in Alwar, Rajasthan.

We recently finished our first pilot in Alwar, Rajasthan. Our solution helped over Thousand mothers. We observerd increase in medical compliance and got valuable feedback to help improve our solution.
